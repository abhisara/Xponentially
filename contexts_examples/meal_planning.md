# Meal Planning Context

## Dietary Preferences
- Vegetarian diet
- No dairy allergies
- Prefer Mediterranean and Indian cuisine
- Moderate spice tolerance
- Focus on whole foods and minimal processing

## Meal Prep Schedule
- **Sunday**: Bulk prep for the week (2-3 hours)
  - Cook grains (quinoa, rice)
  - Prep vegetables
  - Make 2-3 base sauces
- **Wednesday**: Mid-week refresh (30-45 min)
  - Fresh vegetables
  - Quick protein prep

## Typical Week Structure
- **Breakfast**: Simple - oatmeal, smoothies, or toast
- **Lunch**: Meal-prepped bowls or salads
- **Dinner**: Freshly cooked or assembled from prep
- **Snacks**: Fruits, nuts, hummus with veggies

## Common Ingredients to Keep Stocked
### Grains & Legumes
- Quinoa, brown rice, lentils
- Chickpeas (canned and dried)
- Whole wheat pasta

### Proteins
- Tofu, tempeh
- Paneer (occasional)
- Eggs
- Greek yogurt

### Vegetables (Seasonal Rotation)
- Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
- Bell peppers, zucchini
- Tomatoes, onions, garlic
- Seasonal vegetables

### Pantry Essentials
- Olive oil, coconut oil
- Tahini, hummus
- Spices: cumin, turmeric, paprika, garam masala
- Soy sauce, sriracha
- Canned tomatoes, coconut milk

## Recipe Rotation (Favorites)
1. **Mediterranean Buddha Bowl**
   - Quinoa, roasted vegetables, chickpeas, tahini dressing

2. **Lentil Curry**
   - Red lentils, coconut milk, tomatoes, Indian spices

3. **Quinoa Salad**
   - Quinoa, cucumber, tomatoes, feta, lemon vinaigrette

4. **Stir-Fry Bowls**
   - Tofu/tempeh, vegetables, rice, soy-ginger sauce

5. **Chickpea Curry (Chana Masala)**
   - Chickpeas, tomato-based sauce, Indian spices

6. **Veggie Tacos**
   - Black beans, roasted vegetables, salsa, guacamole

## Meal Planning Workflow
1. **Check inventory** - what's in fridge/pantry?
2. **Choose 3-4 base recipes** for the week
3. **Create shopping list** - only buy what's needed
4. **Sunday prep** - batch cook grains, chop vegetables, make sauces
5. **Daily assembly** - mix and match prepped ingredients

## Budget Considerations
- Target: $60-80/week for groceries
- Buy seasonal produce (cheaper and fresher)
- Bulk buy grains and legumes
- Limit specialty items
- Shop sales at Whole Foods/Trader Joe's

## Preferences
- Prefer variety - don't eat the same thing all week
- Like colorful, visually appealing meals
- Need adequate protein in each meal
- Enjoy trying new recipes monthly
- Batch cooking is essential for time management

## Notes
- Favorite grocery stores: Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, local farmers market
- Meal prep containers: Glass containers (4-cup size)
- Best cooking times: Sunday morning, Wednesday evening
- Keep backup meals: frozen veggie burgers, pasta, canned beans
